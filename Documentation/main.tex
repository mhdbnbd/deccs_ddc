% Abstract
\begin{abstract}
    \addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
    This thesis presents DDECCS, a framework that combines consensus-based clustering with interpretable cluster descriptions for attribute-annotated image datasets. The approach integrates two complementary methods: DECCS (Deep Embedded Clustering with Consensus Representations), which improves clustering robustness through ensemble agreement, and DDC (Deep Descriptive Clustering), which generates concise, human-readable cluster explanations via Integer Linear Programming (ILP).

    Our pipeline extracts frozen ResNet-101 features, applies consensus clustering from a heterogeneous ensemble of four algorithms (K-means, Spectral Clustering, GMM, Agglomerative Clustering), and generates orthogonal ILP descriptions---where each semantic attribute is assigned to at most one cluster, ensuring distinctive explanations---using the datasets' semantic attributes. We introduce an adaptive ILP coverage parameter ($\alpha$) that extends DDC's formulation to datasets with sparse binary attributes, where DDC's fixed $\alpha = 8$ is infeasible.

    We evaluate on two established datasets: Animals with Attributes~2 (AwA2, 50 classes, 85 attributes) and Attribute Pascal and Yahoo (aPY, 15/32 classes, 64 attributes). On AwA2, DDECCS achieves NMI = 0.871, competitive with DDC's reported 0.896. On aPY, DECCS consensus provides a 14.4\% improvement over K-means (NMI: 0.534 $\to$ 0.611), demonstrating the value of ensemble clustering on challenging multi-category datasets. ILP descriptions achieve Tag Coverage up to 0.95 and are semantically meaningful, producing descriptions such as ``orange, stripes, meatteeth, claws'' for tiger clusters and ``Wheel, Pedal, Handlebars, Metal'' for bicycle clusters.

    The thesis also documents extensive experiments with alternative approaches---including autoencoder-based training and DDC-style MLP optimization---that informed the final design, providing practical insights into the challenges of multi-objective deep clustering.
\end{abstract}
